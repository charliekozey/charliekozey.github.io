Zero to Mastery: Kicking Things Off

What?

Today I’m kicking off my work on this free guide by Andrei Neagoie: Learn to Code for Free, Get Hired in 5 Months.

Why?

  • Learn in public: Learning is fun, and I want to get better at sharing my process with others
  • Improve: Become a better developer so I can better support my students in their learning and in their job searches, and maybe even get a dev job myself if I decide to do that one day
  • Pre-selected resources: Expose myself to a wide range of sequenced resources that have already been curated (because there’s so much out there to wade through!)
  • Find inspiration: Find examples of teaching techniques and resources to add to my toolbox and/or share with students

How?

I’ve made a simplified version of the guide at this GitHub repo where I can track my progress and keep my practice code organized. There’s also a blank version of the checklist if you’d like to follow along!

Over the coming weeks, I’ll be writing brief check-ins on my progress and sharing them here. My goal is to spend at least 10 minutes every weekday on learning: small and consistent building blocks that will compound over time.

First Up: The Big Picture + HTML + CSS

I’ve already learned a lot of the content in this program, especially in the first month, so I don’t anticipate it will actually take the full length of time. But I really want to shore up my foundations, so I do plan to at least skim each resource or spend a few minutes reviewing it (as in the case of my dear old pal Flexbox Froggy) to see if there are any gaps it can help me fill in.

I plan to spend the bulk of my efforts in this section on computer networking and computer science fundamentals, particularly the Harvard CS50 course, since my training as a bootcamp grad has been pretty nuts-and-bolts. I’m looking forward to diving in to the more conceptual stuff!

I’ll also spend a decent chunk of time on the “How to Learn” section, to make the process a little more efficient, and to identify nuggets of wisdom to share with my students on their learning journeys.
